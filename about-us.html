About DLC

The Distance Learning Centre Limited is a fully state-owned institute functioning under the Ministry of Public Administration, Home Affairs, Provincial Councils, and Local Government. It was established as a World Bank Project in 2002 with counterpart funding from the Government of Sri Lanka offered through a subsidiary loan agreement that was reached between the Ministry of Finance and DLC. With fourteen years of imparting new knowledge to both public & private sector personnel, it has become a credible training venue for many clients of the country.

Sri Lanka has become the first country in South Asia to be linked with the World Bank’s Global Development Learning Network (GDLN) with the establishment of a state-of-the-art Distance Learning Centre in Colombo, Sri Lanka. It has been instrumental in providing global knowledge-sharing experiences and some of those would have influenced the policy-making process in some organizations to carry out institutional changes. Over the past two and half decades, DLC has been able to deliver capacity-building programs to employees in Government and Private Sector organizations to increase their productivity and efficiency.

In addition, its programs to the education sector, have been well received and have enabled the teachers to use ICT in their education initiatives. Through this, a significant number of Principals and Education Managers in the country have acquired updated knowledge and skills in line with best practices in the education sphere at the global level. Moreover, DLC has consistently received favorable feedback from the clients who use the facility. Higher technical support, superior infrastructure, friendly staff, and flexible management processes have contributed to earning this name. However, there is much potential for it to grow, given its strengths and the opportunities that are evolving in Sri Lanka and abroad.

Historical Perspective

The The Distance Learning Centre Limited DLC was established under a World Bank Project in 2002, Located at the Sri Lanka Institute of Development Administration (SLIDA), DLC is funded by a Learning Innovation Loan (LIL) of USD 02 Million from the World Bank and counterpart funds UDS 1 Million from Government of Sri Lanka. It will use a combination of four main technologies, such as Video Conferencing, the Internet, CDROM and Print to conduct Training & Skill Development for Senior Civil Servants and Corporate Executives. Later it was to function as an independent entity and as a Public and Private Partnership as per the understanding reached between the WB and Govt. of Sri Lanka and Centre incorporated under the Companies Act on 01 Nov 2001 and commenced its operation on 26 March 2002.

The Project was managed and operated by Distance Learning Centre Ltd. a Public Limited Liability Company which is a collective enterprise of the Government and Private Sector. The Board of Directors of DLC Ltd. represents both the Public & Private Sectors. Distance Learning Centre assumed special significance as Sri Lanka is poised for an economic turnaround, and in the context of the renewed possibility of Peace.

The core of the Distance Learning Centre comprises a state-of-the-art interactive video classroom the enables interaction between local course participants, remote instructors, and international participants, and a computer laboratory consisting of 02 rooms with 40 personal computers for independent Internetbased interaction among peer groups and instructors. Connectivity has been provided by a dedicated Very Small Aperture Terminal (VSAT) satellite link with the World Bank in Washington DC. DLC was the only one of its kind in Sri Lanka, and the facilities are far superior to any offered by local organizations offering face-to-face or web-based International Training Programs. The Centre built a link between the GDLN, the British Council Network, and JNet (The Japanese Educational Network) to enhance the Centre’s access to information and Training Resources.

The Distance Learning Centre Ltd. Today

The share capital of the company is 14,206,500 shares (Rs.10 each) owned by the Government of Sri Lanka, representing 100% ownership of the government. DLC is also part of the Global Development Learning Network of the World Bank Institute, which provides cost-effective learning opportunities and capacity-building programs through its interactive, multi-channel network, a flexible learning system of two-way videoconferencing, Internet, and other advanced communications technologies.

DLC especially provides global knowledge-sharing opportunities and learning opportunities. The stateof-the-art video conferencing facility and the multimedia labs of DLC enable it to create virtual classrooms where the experts and participants could meet each other in a virtual environment, lively, online or offline modes. Various corporate clients continue to use DLC in conducting their own programs obtaining DLC’s service as a facilitator. Some of the world’s leading Testing bodies such as Pearson VUE and Prometric have begun using DLC facilities extensively. DLC additionally to provide exclusive online services such as; facilitating online interviews, securing meetings between offshore principles and local clients, International arbitration, and home-proctored recruiting exams for government entities. Further international ERP suppliers rent out the training facilities for their offshore user training. DLC over the years has earned a name as a credible training provider, global knowledgesharing partner, and facility provider for both public and private sectors. The past programs designed and developed by Asian Development Bank Institute and a few other international institutes like WHO, ILO, and such are now no longer available. This is also true of its own programs, especially in the ICT and e-learning areas no longer offered by DLC.

With its existence of twenty years of imparting new knowledge to both Public & Private Sector personnel, it has now become a credible Training venue for a large number of clients in the country. DLC has been able to deliver both Academic and Professional Knowledge to the employees in government and private sector organizations to increase their productivity and efficiency. A significant number of principals and education managers in the country have acquired updated knowledge and skills in line with best practices in the education sphere at the global level. Moreover, DLC has received very favorable feedback from the clients who use the facility, consistently. Higher technical support, superior infrastructure, friendly staff, and flexible management processes have contributed to earning this name and it will be continued ever.
