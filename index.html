About Us

The Distance Learning Centre Limited is a fully state-owned institute functioning under the Ministry of Public Administration, Home Affairs, Provincial Councils, and Local Government. It was established as a World Bank Project in 2002 with counterpart funding from the Government of Sri Lanka offered through a subsidiary loan agreement that was reached between the Ministry of Finance and DLC. With fourteen years of imparting new knowledge to both public & private sector personnel, it has become a credible training venue for many clients of the country.

Sri Lanka has become the first country in South Asia to be linked with the World Bank’s Global Development Learning Network (GDLN) with the establishment of a state-of-the-art Distance Learning Centre in Colombo, Sri Lanka. It has been instrumental in providing global knowledge-sharing experiences and some of those would have influenced the policy-making process in some organizations to carry out institutional changes. Over the past two and half decades, DLC has been able to deliver capacity-building programs to employees in Government and Private Sector organizations to increase their productivity and efficiency.

In addition, its programs to the education sector, have been well received and have enabled the teachers to use ICT in their education initiatives. Through this, a significant number of Principals and Education Managers in the country have acquired updated knowledge and skills in line with best practices in the education sphere at the global level. Moreover, DLC has consistently received favorable feedback from the clients who use the facility. Higher technical support, superior infrastructure, friendly staff, and flexible management processes have contributed to earning this name. However, there is much potential for it to grow, given its strengths and the opportunities that are evolving in Sri Lanka and abroad.
